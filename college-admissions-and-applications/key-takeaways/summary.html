College Admissions and College Applications
Summary

The college admissions process can be daunting. However, with some preparation and guidance, it can also be exciting and rewarding. The takeaways readers have encountered in the foregoing chapters of this book can help students and college applicants navigate the process.
Our students believe that the advice gathered in this book can help others improve their chances of finding and getting into the right schools. Our readers can expect to make the most of their college admissions experience by staying organized and proactive (and positive).

Start Early

High school students can start thinking about college as early as freshman year in high school. Students can research the schools that match their interests and academic abilities during their first three years in high school. They can also talk to a school counselor about their college goals.

Know What's Out There

Applicants have many types of institutions from which to choose. Their options include public and private schools, liberal arts colleges, small and large universities, and technical schools. Applicants stand a good chance of finding what suits them best by knowing all their options.

Get Good Grades

Through the end of junior year, grades and test scores will play a significant role in admissions. Taking challenging courses and achieving academic success can make an impact.

Score Big

Most colleges require standardized tests like the SAT or ACT. The application process is very competitive, so students may consider taking a test prep course or studying independently to apply with their best possible scores. It is common for applicants to sit for an exam more than once to improve their scores: some take both college entrance exams to determine the score they want to use in their applications.

Get Involved

Colleges want to see well-rounded students who are involved in their community and have various interests. Consider joining clubs or sports teams, volunteering, employment, or pursuing hobbies that showcase your talents.

Personal Statements Matter

The personal statement is your chance to showcase your personality and highlight your accomplishments. Spend time crafting a well-written and engaging essay. Engaging a reader in your shared human experience is a powerful opportunity to create an impactful application.

Letters of Recommendation

Many colleges require letters of recommendation—whether from teachers or other adults who know students and applicants well. Choosing people who can speak to one’s academic abilities, character, and work ethic is essential.

Submitting on Time

College can be expensive, so it is a good idea to research financial aid options early on. Students can search for scholarships, grants, and other aid to make college more affordable.

Following Up

Know and submit: know the applications materials deadlines for each school and submit those materials well in advance

Financial Aid

After submission, students and applicants should consider a follow-up. This way, they can ensure that every school they have applied to has received exactly what it specifies it needs. Keep track of any deadlines or additional materials that may be required.
In sum, when it comes to the college admissions process, the stakes can be high; the process itself can be complicated; and, the competition can be great. The experience may therefore tax students and applicants emotionally as well as physically. The financial implications of consuming a college education can also weigh on individuals and their families. Whatever students face, it is our hope that they will be able to confidently approach college admissions with greater understanding and ease thanks to the information in this book.
