Section I: Key Takeaways
Start Early

“Time is what keeps everything from happening at once.”
Ray Cummings
High school students can start thinking about college at any time. Freshman year, or ninth grade, is as good a time to start as any.
To begin, students can:
  • research schools with proper guidance
  • spend their first three high school years looking for schools that match their interests and academic abilities
  • talk with their school counselor.
Moreover, it is an especially good idea for students to talk with a counselor at school about their college readiness and their college goals.
Starting the college admissions process early can help applicants avoid feeling rushed or stressed as deadlines approach. By giving themselves plenty of time, applicants can carefully research schools, prepare for standardized tests, gather materials, and write strong application essays.
When it comes to college applications and the college admissions process, it is a good idea to start early. The sooner, the better, as they say.
