Section I: Key Takeaways
Score Big

“'You have been in Afghanistan, I perceive.' 'How on earth did you know that?'” [Sherlock Holmes' first encounter with Dr. Watson]
Arthur Conan Doyle
Some colleges are test-optional and still accept standardized test scores from tests like the SAT or the ACT. The application process is very competitive, so students may consider taking a test prep course or studying independently to apply with their best possible scores.
It is common for applicants to sit for an exam more than once to improve their scores: some take both available college entrance exams to determine the score they want to use in their applications.
Curiously, students often receive advice or feel pressure to take these important tests without preparation. But because good scores are so important, then surely they are worth studying for.
In addition to posting an excellent score for admission, many colleges factor the score into scholarship offers. Thus, it can be an integral part of the decision-making process for a student seeking funding at a selected school.
Getting a good score on standardized testing (SAT/ACT) is incredibly important. However, people still take these tests “cold”—or, without study or preparation. Students should study, train, and “score big” on standardized testing.
